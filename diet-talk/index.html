What is Sciatica?

Sciatica is no joke, but unfortunately more than 3 million people a year in the US alone suffer from the immense shooting pain caused by this condition. This pain originates in the spine but can radiate all the way down to the back of the leg, making simple things like walking or getting in and out of a chair an absolutely horrifying ordeal.

People who haven’t suffered from sciatica may think that I’m exaggerating, but I most certainly am not...

Sciatica happens when a herniated disk or bone spur in the spine presses against a nerve, triggering a reaction that causes great pain, generally in one side of the body.

Symptoms of sciatica include pain in the back, buttocks, hip, or other lower extremities.

The pain can range from mild to severe and may be sharp and radiating. Some people with Sciatica experience leg numbness or pins and needles in the affected areas. Other common symptoms include burning sensations in the body and muscle weakness.

Left untreated, these symptoms can become worse and contribute to other conditions that bring on even more pain and discomfort.
